As our pick for best laptop for programming, the Apple MacBook Pro 14-inch is well-equipped to tackle coding and software engineering projects. This, after all, is a device capable of professional-grade photo and video editing. Better still, you can boot it in Windows, too, giving you a bit more flexibility.

The latest version packs an M4 Pro processor, which we found offered incredible performance over comparable laptops. In tests, Geekbench 6.3 numbers easily exceeded those of our MacBook Air M3. Image editing was a breeze, but while the laptop handled editing four 4K 30fps at once, we noted frame-rate drops when we bumped that to 4K 120fps video clips shot on the iPhone 16 Pro Max. As such, the M4 MacBook Pro shouldn't have any issues running programming applications. If you prefer a larger screen, check out our Apple MacBook Pro 16-inch (M4 Pro, 2024) review.

Alright, the design is the same as the last version, but why break what's not broken? Overall, we found the Apple MacBook Pro 14-inch delivers breathtaking power, portability, and an XDR display with 1600 nits peak brightness. Port selection is good, including an SD card slot, an HDMI port, and three Thunderbolt 4 ports. We also found battery life to be exceptional, lasting over 18 hours on a single charge in our battery tests.

Whether you need a machine with this sort of power will depend on the complexity of your workloads, but it’s a beautiful device with outstanding performance across the board. For cheaper programmer laptops, the M1 and M2 MacBook Pro laptops still get our seal of approval.

The Lenovo LOQ 15-inch is a solid mid-range gaming laptop with impressive performance for its price. Equipped with an Intel Core i5/i7 processor and NVIDIA RTX graphics, it can handle both gaming and productivity tasks with ease.

The 144Hz IPS display ensures smooth visuals, making it a great choice for gamers. In our tests, the Lenovo LOQ managed to run most modern titles at high settings without major frame drops. The build quality is sturdy, though the design remains quite simple compared to premium gaming laptops.

Battery life is decent but not groundbreaking, lasting around 6-7 hours with mixed usage. However, if you’re primarily using it for gaming, expect to keep it plugged in most of the time. The laptop also features a well-spaced keyboard and a responsive trackpad, making it suitable for both gaming and everyday work.

Overall, the Lenovo LOQ 15-inch is an excellent choice for budget-conscious gamers who want strong performance without breaking the bank.

The Acer Aspire 5 is a budget-friendly laptop that delivers solid performance for everyday tasks like web browsing, document editing, and media consumption. It comes with both Intel and AMD processor options, offering decent power at an affordable price.

While not designed for gaming, the optional NVIDIA RTX 2050 GPU allows light gaming and content creation. The build quality is solid, and the laptop has a professional design suitable for work and study.

Battery life is a strong point, lasting up to 8 hours on a single charge under normal usage. The keyboard is comfortable, and the trackpad is responsive, making it a good choice for students and professionals looking for a reliable daily driver.

Overall, the Acer Aspire 5 is a great option for users looking for a balance between performance, affordability, and portability. It's not a powerhouse, but it gets the job done efficiently.
